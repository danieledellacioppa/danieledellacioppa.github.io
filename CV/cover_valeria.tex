{\large\bfseries\color{ink}Customer Service Supervisor Cover Letter}

\vspace{4pt}

Dear Jerry,

I am writing to express my interest in the Customer Service Supervisor role. I feel that my previous leadership experience, together with everything I have learned over the past months at Uniqlo, could be a great asset to the Supervisor team and beneficial to the agents. I genuinely believe Uniqlo is a great company, and I would welcome the opportunity to show my best, contribute further and continue to grow professionally.

Based on my understanding of the EU Customer Service environment, a supervisor is responsible for helping agents deliver a consistent, high-quality customer experience across multiple European markets while balancing empathy, service quality, SOPs, GDPR, consumer rights and brand values. The role also means owning escalations, coaching the team, keeping knowledge and operational processes clear, and protecting wellbeing so that busy periods do not lead to burnout.

Although I have not been an agent for very long, I am a quick learner, highly self-motivated and always keen to lead by example. I also understand that when you support agents, you start to see the wider picture: the challenges people face day to day, the limits and opportunities within the business, and the need to make decisions that are fair for both the customer and the company.

My previous roles as a Restaurant Manager, my eight years as an Associate Team Leader at Whole Foods, and my experience as a teacher have taught me how to create a positive environment where people feel included, trusted and part of the team.

In my current role at Uniqlo, I have already had the opportunity to contribute beyond my daily duties. I was awarded Agent of the Month, I work confidently with VOC and TCD, I have presented information during Team Talks, trained both individuals and groups of agents, checked dispositions and order numbers, and supported the team with translation tasks. These experiences have helped me understand the needs of agents more clearly and have strengthened my confidence in supporting others.

I am also comfortable taking ownership of escalations and complex cases. As a Duty Manager, I was often responsible for the whole shop and had to deal with challenging situations where calm judgement and clear decision-making were essential. That experience has made me confident in de-escalating situations, listening carefully and finding solutions that work for both the customer and the business while staying within policy.

I understand that in EU Customer Service, agents need clear guidance so that SOPs, GDPR, consumer rights regulations and brand values are applied correctly. In my previous roles, I kept processes up to date, developed systems to track daily tasks and product knowledge, and helped the team understand what was expected of them.

As an Associate Team Leader, I learned the importance of being a second pair of eyes, stepping up when needed and helping the Market Leader keep things running smoothly. I have carried out one-to-one reviews, monitored performance and used constructive feedback, coaching and leading by example to help people grow.

Thank you for considering my application. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience in team leadership, operations, coaching and customer service could support the team and help me succeed as a Customer Service Supervisor.

\vspace{4pt}

Kind regards,

\textbf{Valeria Kierbel}
